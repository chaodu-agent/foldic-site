FAQ

Does Foldic modify my Photos library?

No. Foldic is strictly one-way: it reads your albums through Apple's PhotoKit and writes files to a folder you choose. It never edits, deletes, or reorganizes anything in Photos.

Where do my photos go?

To a folder you pick — on your Mac, an external drive, or a NAS mount. Each album becomes its own subfolder of original-quality files.

What about Live Photos?

Both parts are exported: the still image and the paired video file.

How fast do iPhone photos show up?

As soon as iCloud syncs them to your Mac. Keeping Photos.app running makes iCloud deliver faster; Foldic also re-checks your library every few minutes.

What happens if I remove or delete a photo in Apple Photos?

It depends on where you remove it. If you remove a photo from an album, it remains in your Photos Library and in any other albums. After Foldic scans again, only the exported copy in that album’s folder is marked as orphaned.

If you delete a photo from your Photos Library, Apple moves it to Recently Deleted and removes that photo from every album that contains it. After Foldic scans again, all affected exported copies — including copies in album folders and, if enabled, the All Photos mirror — are marked as orphaned.

Foldic never deletes exported files automatically. You decide whether to keep or remove orphaned files.

Does Foldic collect any data?

None. No analytics, no telemetry, no account. Read the full privacy policy.
